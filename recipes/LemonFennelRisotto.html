Lemon Fennel Risotto

Description

Risotto is one of my dad’s specialties—he had endless variations, but I always found myself drawn to his lemon risotto. Determined to put my own spin on it, I experimented with different vegetables until I landed on fennel. Its subtle sweetness and slight crunch add the perfect contrast to the creamy rice, making this dish bright, comforting, and full of texture.

This recipe follows the classic way to do risotto and offers a fresh alternative to the classic mushroom and saffron risotto. I start by sauteing some onion in a pan with chopped fennel, add the risotto rice and cook it slowly in vegetable broth. Then I finish it with some mascarpone, parmesan cheese, and lemon juice and zest.

Ingredients

  • 1 large fennel, chopped
  • ½ onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • ½ tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tbsp butter
  • 2 cups Arborio rice
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • 6 cups hot vegetable stock
  • 1 lemon (zest and juice)
  • ½ cup parmesan cheese, grated
  • 1 tbsp mascarpone (or sour cream)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Step-by-Step Recipe

  1. Prepare and heat your broth and make sure to keep it hot.
  2. Chop the fennel in bite-size pieces and the onion and the garlic in small pieces.
  3. Heat the olive oil and butter on medium in a pot, and fry the garlic and onion until lightly browned. Add the chopped fennel and fry for another minute. Add the uncooked risotto rice, stirring constantly until the rice becomes translucent.
  4. Add some of the vegetable stock that you prepared beforehand, and let it cook uncovered to a simmer.
  5. Stir every two minutes, adding more broth to cover the risotto rice as it absorbs the liquids. Repeat until you use all the stock, and the rice will be cooked. This will take you approximately 20 minutes.
  6. Once your rice is cooked through, add the lemon zest and juice, the parmesan cheese, the mascarpone, and salt & pepper to taste.
  7. Turn off the heat and let your risotto sit for 2-3 minutes so that it can reach the right consistency. Serve & enjoy.
