NY Bagels

Description

There is nothing more comforting than a doughy bagel in the morning for me, but coming back from New York last winter, I had to face the hard truth in California: while we do have lots of good foods, bagels are definitely not reaching NYC’s quality and taste. So I decided to reproduce them in my kitchen!

NYC Bagels History

Bagels have a long and rich history in New York City. They were first introduced by Jewish immigrants in the late 19th century and quickly became a staple of the city's food culture. The traditional New York bagel is made with high-quality flour, water, and salt, and is boiled before being baked to create its distinctive chewy texture and golden-brown crust.

Ingredients

For the yeast:

  • ½ cup of water (105-115 F/ 40-46 C)
  • 4 and ½ tsp of sugar
  • 2 tsp of dry active yeast

For the dough:

  • 440g of bread flour
  • 6g (1 tsp) of salt
  • ⅓ + ½ a cup of water

Step-by-Step Recipe

  1. In a mixing bowl, pour the yeast mixture and let it sit at room temperature for about 10 minutes until it bubbles. Don’t stir.
  2. Mix flour and salt in a large mixing bowl, make a well in the center and pour the yeast mixture.
  3. Pour ⅓ cup of water into the well and the remaining ½ cup as needed. Careful not to have a dough that is too moist! You want a dry dough. It will eventually get smoother as you knead.
  4. Knead the dough for about 20-30 minutes until smooth and elastic. You can do this by hand or using a stand mixer.
  5. Let the dough rise in a bowl lightly brushed with oil and cover with a damp towel at room temperature. This might take between 30 minutes to an hour depending on the environment. You’ll know that your dough is ready when you try to poke it and it does not rise back.
  6. Once proofed, punch your dough to deflate it.
  7. Split your dough into 8 equal parts and start shaping the bagels by flattening the dough and rolling it. You can also shape the dough in small balls and poke a hole in the middle.
  8. Let the shaped bagels rise in your refrigerator overnight (18 hours is the sweet spot) or 2 hours at room temperature.
  9. The next morning, fill a medium pan with water, add 2 tbsp of malt syrup, and bring it to a simmer.
  10. Boil your bagels in simmering water for about 30 seconds on each side.
  11. Cover them with egg wash and add any desired toppings.
  12. Bake for about 25 minutes at 425 degrees Fahrenheit.
  13. Let the bagels cool down, slice, fill, and enjoy.
