Easy and Comforting Sausage Cassoulet

Description

I used to eat canned cassoulet as a kid, and as weird as it might sound that was one of my all time favorites. This recipe is a little tweak on Jacques Pepin's Sausage Cassoulet recipe. It is warm, comforting, rich, and packed with protein. History of cassoulet

Cassoulet is a French stew made of white beans, meat, and seasoning. Its first version dates back from the medieval age during the Hundred years war and legends say it was a way to feed the soldiers coming back from the war field.

There is a pretty big debate behind the history of the cassoulet, with three main French cities arguing about its origin: Castelnaudray, Carcassonne, and Toulouse. This led to multiple variations in terms of meat in this dish. While some recipes call for duck, others use pork shoulder, sausages, and even goose confit.

So what makes a cassoulet a cassoulet? The two constant variables are the white beans and the presence of meat, but the possibilities are endless, which is why I love this dish. It is hearty, filling, and versatile!

Ingredients

For the croutons:

  • 2 slices of toast bread (I like using whole wheat)
  • 1 tbsp of toasted peanut oil

For the stew:

  • 4 oz (115g) of pancetta
  • 12 oz (340g) of Italian sausages (about 4)
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 a white onion (if big) or 1 small one
  • A bunch of green onions (I use around 5)
  • 2 cans of cannellini beans (with their juice)
  • 1 tsp (about 6g) of Italian seasoning
  • 10 splashes of Tabasco sauce

Step-by-Step Recipe

For the croutons:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375 degree Fahrenheit (190 C).
  2. Cut your bread slices in small cubes and coat them with peanut oil. You can also use a neutral oil, olive oil, or butter deepening on your taste. Feel free to season as you'd like (I personally just use peanut oil since it's already very flavorful).
  3. Bake for 15-20 minutes. Depending on your oven, they might need a little less time, so I would check at the 10-minutes mark to make sure they don't burn. Set aside.

For the stew:

  1. Chop the garlic, onion, and scallions. I like to make it very thin so that it fully blends in with the cassoulet. Slice the sausages into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Fry the pancetta in a saucepan on medium-heat and let it release its fat content.
  3. Add the onion, garlic, and scallions and fry for about 2 minutes until they get soft and brown.
  4. Add the sliced sausage, stirring to coat them in the pancetta drippings. Cover with the lid and let it cook on medium-low heat for 5 minutes. It should simmer, not boil! Stir occasionally to ensure even cooking and prevent burning.
  5. Once the sausages are cooked, add in the cannellini beans with their juice. Cover for another 2 minutes to reheat the beans. You want to make sure not to cook too long so that your beans don’t get too mushy––unless you like them this way.
  6. Add the tabasco and the Italian seasoning and top with the peanut croutons. VOILA!
